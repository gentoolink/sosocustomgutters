Leaf Gutter Guards & Gutter Protection

If you're tired of climbing a ladder every spring and fall, gutter guards are the solution. The right system keeps out leaves, needles, and debris while letting water flow freely into the eavestrough.

Why Kelowna Properties Need Gutter Guards

The Okanagan has no shortage of trees — cottonwoods and aspens line neighborhoods throughout Kelowna and West Kelowna, and they're dropping leaves, seeds, and fluff from early spring through late autumn. Pine needles accumulate in gutters year-round. Without protection, your eavestrough clogs with an organic mat that holds water, breeds mosquitoes, and adds weight that stresses your brackets.

What Leaf Guards Prevent

  • Clogged downspouts causing water to overflow and damage landscaping
  • Ice dams forming on top of debris-filled gutters in winter
  • Pine needles matting together and blocking all water flow
  • Birds and wasps nesting in gutter debris
  • Premature bracket and hanger failure from excess weight

Our Gutter Guard Options

We offer two main approaches depending on your roof, budget, and tree situation:

  • Mesh leaf covers — aluminum or plastic mesh panels that sit on top of the gutter. Water passes through; leaves and needles sit on top and blow off or dry out. Best for pine needles and small debris.
  • Solid gutter helmets — curved covers where water clings to the surface and flows into a small opening along the front edge. Best for heavy leaf fall. More expensive but virtually maintenance-free.

Installation on Existing Gutters

We can add leaf guards to most existing continuous eavestrough systems without removing the current setup. If your gutters are old, sagging, or rusted, we'll tell you honestly whether guards make sense or whether full replacement is the better investment.

New Installation with Guards Pre-Installed

When we install new eavestrough, we can build the guard system into the quote. New installs with guards cost less than retrofits — ask us for a line-item comparison.

Guard Systems We Offer

Mesh Leaf Covers
  • Aluminum or UV-stabilized plastic mesh
  • Sits flat or slightly angled on top of gutter
  • Handles pine needles, seeds, and small leaves well
  • Lower cost, simple installation
  • Occasional maintenance — brush off top occasionally
Solid Gutter Helmets
  • Curved aluminum panels covering the opening
  • Water follows the curve and enters through a front slot
  • Leaves and debris sit on top and dry out / blow away
  • Higher upfront cost, minimal ongoing maintenance
  • Best for heavy tree coverage — cottonwood and aspen properties
