Common Gutter Problems We Fix

Water damage from a failing gutter system is among the most expensive home repairs that homeowners in the Okanagan face — foundation cracks, basement flooding, and siding damage can run into tens of thousands of dollars. A leaking or sagging gutter that's ignored for one season can cause damage that takes years to discover. We fix problems fast, correctly, and with materials that last.

Leaking Seams and Joints

Sectional gutter systems develop leaks at the seams where individual sections meet — and in the Okanagan's freeze-thaw climate, those leaks tend to appear exactly when you can't properly fix them (mid-winter). We seal leaking seams with aluminum-compatible adhesive, replace damaged mitered corners, and where the damage is severe enough, we recommend upgrading to seamless eavestrough. A properly sealed seam should last 5-8 years in Okanagan conditions; if you're re-sealing the same joint every year, it's time to replace the run.

Sagging Gutters

Sagging is almost always a mounting problem. Spike-and-ferrule systems (the nails hammered into the fascia) work loose over time, especially in the Okanagan where thermal expansion and contraction is severe. When a gutter sags, water pools in the sag rather than flowing to the downspout — which accelerates deterioration, causes overflow in heavy rain, and creates ice dams in winter. We rehang sagging gutters with Alu-Rex T-Rex continuous hanger systems, which distribute weight evenly and prevent future sagging without removing the existing eavestrough in most cases.

Downspout Issues

Downspouts that are disconnected, crushed, or improperly positioned cause some of the most insidious foundation damage — because it happens slowly, underground, and out of sight. We replace damaged downspouts, adjust elbow positions to direct water at least 4 feet from your foundation, and install underground extension systems for properties where surface drainage won't solve the problem.

Ice Dam Damage

The Okanagan's freeze-thaw cycle in winter creates ice dams that push gutters away from fascia, damage the gutter itself, and sometimes tear the gutter mounting brackets clean off the house. We repair ice dam damage in spring — fixing the bent aluminum, re-hanging the gutter system, and installing heating cable in severe cases to prevent recurrence. If you've had ice dam issues, it's worth discussing T-Rex continuous hangers, which reinforce the front edge of the gutter against this type of damage.

Fascia and Soffit Repair

Before we hang any new gutter system, we inspect the fascia. If the fascia boards are rotted or soft, the gutter cannot be mounted securely — new hangers driven into soft wood will pull out within a season. We identify and flag fascia issues before installation, and we can arrange repairs through our network of trusted contractors if the damage is extensive. Minor fascia repairs (replacing 1-2 boards) are handled as part of our installation service.

Emergency Repairs

If a storm has torn your gutter system loose or a tree has fallen on your eavestrough, call us. We prioritize emergency calls and can usually send someone the same day to make a temporary fix that stops further damage — then follow up with a permanent repair on your schedule.
