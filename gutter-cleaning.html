Why Annual Gutter Cleaning Matters in the Okanagan

The Okanagan's climate makes gutter maintenance non-negotiable. Autumn brings heavy leaf fall across the valley — cottonwood, maple, oak, and poplar all drop their canopy into your eavestrough at the same time. Winter brings ice damming when debris-blocked gutters freeze. Spring sends the snowmelt onto your roof because water can't reach the downspouts. Then summer's dry conditions bake the accumulated organic matter into a fire hazard and a bacterial culture.

Properties with mature trees within 30 feet of the roofline should have their gutters cleaned twice per year — once after leaf fall (November-December) and once in early spring (March-April). Properties with less tree exposure can often manage with a single annual clean, but we assess each property individually.

What's Included in Our Gutter Cleaning

Full Debris Removal

We hand-remove all debris from the eavestrough channel — leaves, needles, seed pods, dirt accumulation, and any organic matter that's settled in since the last clean. We don't just blow it out — we scoop it, bag it, and haul it away.

Downspout Flushing

Blocked downspouts are the most common cause of overflow damage that's misdiagnosed as a gutter problem. We flush every downspout from the top, using high-pressure water to clear the inside of the pipe. If a downspout is severely blocked, we identify the location and clear it — which sometimes requires disassembly.

System Inspection

While we're on site, we inspect the full gutter system: checking for loose mounting, identifying leaks at seams and corners, noting any sagging or deformation, and flagging downspout positioning issues. You receive a written inspection summary with photos — including close-up shots of any problem areas we find.

Before/After Photo Documentation

Every cleaning includes before and after photos of the eavestrough interior. These are emailed to you and available for review on request. This documentation matters if you're ever filing a home insurance claim related to water damage — you have evidence the system was maintained.

Commercial Gutter Maintenance

For property managers, strata corporations, and commercial building owners — we offer annual maintenance contracts that cover all owned gutter systems on a scheduled basis. This eliminates the per-call overhead and ensures your properties are maintained before problems arise rather than after. Contact us for commercial pricing.

Pricing

Residential gutter cleaning starts at $4.50 per linear foot for standard single-family homes. Multi-story homes, properties with significant tree coverage, and homes with 6-inch gutters are priced accordingly. We provide a fixed quote before work begins — no surprises on the invoice.
