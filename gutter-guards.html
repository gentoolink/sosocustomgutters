Stop Cleaning Gutters — Let Gutter Guards Do the Work

Properties in the Okanagan with significant tree coverage — pine, fir, cottonwood, poplar, or deciduous trees with large canopies — spend hundreds of dollars every year on gutter cleaning, and still deal with overflow and ice damming when the system gets overwhelmed. Gutter guards and leaf screens eliminate the bulk of debris entering the eavestrough, dramatically reducing cleaning frequency and extending the life of your gutter system.

We're not talking about the cheap plastic snap-on covers you find at the hardware store — those tend to either let fine debris through (which then creates a sludge layer in the gutter) or they create a new surface for pine needles to mat against, making things worse. We install properly engineered gutter protection systems that handle the specific debris types common to Okanagan properties.

Types of Gutter Guards We Install

Mesh Screen Guards

Fine stainless steel or aluminum mesh that fits over the top of the eavestrough, allowing water through while blocking needles, leaves, and larger debris. Effective for pine and fir needles, poplar fluff, and standard leaf fall. Mesh guards are the most cost-effective solution for most Okanagan properties and work well with both sectional and seamless gutters.

Surface Tension Guards (Reverse Curve)

These guards use the surface tension of water — water adheres to the curved surface and flows into the gutter while debris falls off the edge. Effective in areas with heavy leaf fall but not ideal for pine needles, which tend to stick to the curved surface. Best suited to properties with predominantly deciduous tree coverage.

Brush-Style Inserts

Heavy-bristle brushes placed inside the eavestrough channel — debris sits on top of the brushes while water flows around them. Lower initial cost but more maintenance — debris accumulates on top of the brushes and the brushes themselves need periodic cleaning. Appropriate for low-debris situations or as a temporary solution.

Which System Is Right for Your Property?

The right system depends on your tree coverage:

  • Pine/Fir trees: Mesh guards with fine screen — the small needles pass through most other systems and will mat against anything solid
  • Cottonwood/Poplar: Mesh guards or surface-tension guards — seasonal fluff and seed pods are light and will wash over or through most systems
  • Deciduous (maple, oak, birch): Mesh or surface-tension — leaves are large enough that most guards handle them well
  • Mixed tree coverage: Mesh guards as the most versatile solution for multiple debris types

The Cost-Benefit of Gutter Guards

Consider: the average gutter cleaning in the Okanagan costs $150-$300 per visit, and most properties with tree coverage need cleaning twice per year — fall and spring. That's $300-$600 per year, indefinitely. A properly installed gutter guard system costs $12-$20 per linear foot installed, depending on the system chosen. For a typical single-family home with 180 linear feet of eavestrough, that's roughly $2,160-$3,600 — recouped in 3-7 years of avoided cleaning costs, plus the guard extends the life of your gutter system by reducing corrosion and weight load.
