Gutter Services in Vernon, BC

Vernon sits at the crossroads of the Okanagan and North Okanagan, with a climate that demands reliable eavestrough systems year-round. Summers can be hot and dry — followed by heavy snowfall in winter. That freeze-thaw cycle puts real stress on gutters: water pools, ice dams form, and seams weaken. We install continuous seamless eavestrough on-site, made to fit your exact roofline so water flows where it should and stays out of your foundation.

If you're in Kalamazoo, Predator Ridge, or the East Hill area, you're probably dealing with one of two common problems: pine needles and conifer debris clogging standard sectional gutters, or aging systems from the 90s that were never installed properly. We see a lot of overflow issues near the lake — especially on homes with older roofing that has settled slightly over the years.

Vernon-Specific Climate Considerations

The North Okanagan gets more snow accumulation than Kelowna and experiences deeper freeze cycles in winter. This means:

  • Standard spike-and-ferrule gutter systems are more prone to pulling away from fascia in Vernon
  • Ice damming is common on south-facing roof slopes — proper slope and drainage is critical
  • Homes near Kalamazoo Lake or Swan Lake see higher moisture exposure and need higher-capacity systems

We install every system with these local conditions in mind. Our aluminum is 0.027 gauge minimum — heavy enough for the North Okanagan climate, light enough not to stress your fascia. Every joint is sealed with purpose-built adhesive that stays flexible through freeze-thaw cycles.
