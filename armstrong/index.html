Gutter Services in Armstrong, BC

Armstrong is a small city with a mix of established older neighbourhoods and newer developments, and its agricultural heritage means many properties have larger roof spans and open gutter exposure. The North Okanagan climate — cold, damp winters with significant snowfall and warm, dry summers — puts distinct demands on eavestrough systems. Properties near the riverbottom areas see more frost and ground moisture, while homes on the hillside get more wind-driven rain and debris.

We see a lot of aging sectional gutters in Armstrong's older neighbourhoods — the kind installed in the 80s and 90s with mismatched seams and inconsistent slope. These systems were never designed for the volume of water the Okanagan can produce in a heavy storm. We replace them with continuous seamless eavestrough that handles the full catchment without leaking at joints.

Armstrong & North Okanagan Climate Considerations

The North Okanagan, including Armstrong and Enderby, sees some of the coldest temperatures in the Okanagan Valley and the most prolonged snow cover. Key factors for gutter performance in this region:

  • Cold-air pooling in low-lying areas causes ice buildup on north-facing rooflines — we install with extra slope to compensate and recommend heated cable in severe cases
  • Agricultural properties with larger roof spans experience higher water volume per downspout — we size systems accordingly (6-inch for large-span roofs)
  • Mature cottonwood and poplar trees drop heavy seasonal debris — leaf guards significantly reduce maintenance frequency
  • Older homes with wooden fascia boards require careful mounting — we inspect fascia condition and recommend repairs before installation

We make regular service runs to Armstrong and Enderby, and we're familiar with the specific roofing styles common to the region — including the gambrel roofs and agricultural buildings that require custom solutions.
