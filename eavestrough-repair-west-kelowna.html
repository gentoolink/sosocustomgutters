Common Problems We Fix

Kelowna's combination of dry summers, heavy spring rains, and freezing winters takes a toll on eavestrough systems. Here are the issues we see most often:

  • Leaking seams — water escaping at corners and joints, often caused by failed sealant or worn gaskets
  • Sagging gutters — brackets pulled away from the fascia, usually from ice weight or debris buildup
  • Detached downspouts — pop rivets or brackets failed, causing the downspout to pull away from the wall
  • Improper slope — gutters that hold water instead of draining, leading to rust and mosquito breeding
  • Ice dams — freeze-thaw cycles in Okanagan winters back water up under the shingles
  • Overflowing — gutters that were never sized correctly for the roof area

Repair vs. Replace — How to Decide

Repair If:
  • System is under 15 years old
  • Only one or two problem areas
  • Downspouts and hangers are still solid
  • No widespread rust or rot
Replace If:
  • System is over 20 years old
  • Multiple leaks across many runs
  • Widespread rust through or rot in fascia
  • Frequent ice damming despite repairs

Not sure? Call us — we'll honestly tell you whether repair or replacement makes more sense. No upsell pressure.
