Why Seamless Eavestrough Outperforms Sectional Gutters

Every joint in a sectional gutter system is a potential leak point. A standard 40-foot run of sectional gutter has approximately 13 joints — each one a seam where expansion, contraction, and debris buildup eventually cause failures. Seamless eavestrough eliminates those failure points by forming one continuous piece from corner to corner, with only mitered joints at the corners where they're properly sealed and supported.

We form and install seamless aluminum eavestrough on-site at your property. The machine comes to your home or business, we measure your exact roofline, and the eavestrough is fabricated to fit — every downspout location, every corner, every story height accounted for before a single bracket is placed.

The SOS Custom Gutters Installation Process

  1. On-site measurement — We measure the full perimeter of your roof, account for architectural features, and calculate the correct slope toward each downspout.
  2. Material selection — We use 0.027-gauge aluminum as standard (0.032 for commercial or high-snow-load applications). Standard colors include Hunter Green, Midnight Black, Charcoal Grey, Classic Bronze, and Colonial Cream. Custom colors available on request.
  3. On-site fabrication — The machine rolls and forms the eavestrough to your exact measurements — no pre-cut sections, no waste.
  4. Continuous hanger installation — We install Alu-Rex T-Rex continuous hanger systems. These are not spike-and-ferrule — they support the entire length of the gutter, distributing weight evenly and preventing the sagging and pulling away from fascia that plagues older systems.
  5. Sealed corners and joints — Mitered corners are sealed with aluminum-compatible adhesive and mechanically fastened. No silicone-only seals at critical joints.
  6. Downspout installation — We install properly sized downspouts with elbow extensions to direct water at least 4 feet from your foundation. Splash blocks included as standard.

Alu-Rex T-Rex Continuous Hangers — What They Are and Why They Matter

T-Rex is a continuous hanger system made by Alu-Rex, a Canadian manufacturer based in Quebec. Unlike traditional spike-and-ferrule fasteners (the nails hammered through the gutter into the fascia), T-Rex hangers are continuous bars that run inside the gutter and bolt to the fascia at 24-inch intervals. The benefits:

  • Eliminates the "saw tooth" sag that happens when individual fasteners loosen over time
  • Handles snow and ice load without deforming — the gutter literally cannot sag because the continuous bar prevents it
  • Reinforces the front edge of the gutter, preventing the buckling that happens during heavy ice damming
  • Allows water to flow freely since there are no internal brackets blocking the channel

5-Inch vs 6-Inch — Which Do You Need?

5-inch gutters handle the vast majority of residential applications in the Okanagan. We recommend 6-inch gutters when:

  • Your roof has a large catchment area (tall rooflines, multi-story, or steep pitch)
  • Your property sits in a high-precipitation zone (Shuswap, North Okanagan)
  • You have a metal or shake roof that sheds debris at higher volume
  • You have a flat or low-slope roof that doesn't shed water quickly
